PART A - CASE STUDY
UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting people in India, especially students and new digital users. In a typical case, a college student tries to sell an item online through a platform like OLX. A fraudster contacts the student pretending to be a genuine buyer and agrees to pay immediately.
The scammer then sends a fake payment request or QR code and asks the student to “accept” it to receive money. In reality, instead of receiving money, the student unknowingly authorizes a payment from their own account. Sometimes, the fraudster also calls and creates urgency, guiding the victim step-by-step to complete the transaction quickly without thinking.
Students, elderly people, and those unfamiliar with digital payments are usually targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI works. The consequences can be serious, including loss of money, stress, and sometimes sharing of sensitive banking details.
Such frauds happen mainly due to lack of awareness and trust in strangers online. People often assume that receiving money requires entering a PIN, which is false. In reality, a UPI PIN is only needed to send money. Understanding this basic rule and being cautious while dealing with unknown people can help prevent such scams.



PART B - PREVENTION CHECKLIST:
1: Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or passwords with anyone
2: Always verify unknown links before clicking
3:Avoid downloading apps from untrusted sources
4; Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
5: Do not trust strangers on social media or marketplaces
6:Check website URLs carefully before entering details

 UPI / Financial Safety Tips
7: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money
8: Do not scan QR codes sent by unknown people
